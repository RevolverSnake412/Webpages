The Cool Romero

Alfonso John Romero

Doom slayer's buddy

Romero's Timeline:

  • 1967: John Romero is born on October 28 in Colorado Springs, Colorado, USA.
  • 1980: Romero begins programming video games and develops a passion for game design.
  • 1987: Romero creates the platform game "Dangerous Dave," which gains popularity among gamers.
  • 1990: Romero co-founds id Software along with John Carmack, Adrian Carmack, and Tom Hall.
  • 1992: Romero serves as the lead designer for "Wolfenstein 3D," a pioneering first-person shooter game.
  • 1993: He works as the project lead and designer for the iconic game "Doom," which revolutionizes the gaming industry and becomes a cultural phenomenon.
  • 1995: Romero resigns from id Software and establishes Ion Storm, a game development company.
  • 2000: Ion Storm releases "Daikatana," a highly anticipated game that faces numerous delays and mixed reviews.
  • 2001: Romero leaves Ion Storm after its financial struggles and development issues.
  • 2002: He co-founds Monkeystone Games and develops mobile games.
  • 2004: Romero collaborates with his wife Brenda Brathwaite on the game "Ravenwood Fair."
  • 2012: He starts the social gaming company Loot Drop, focusing on social and mobile games.
  • 2015: Romero and Adrian Carmack announce a new FPS game titled "Blackroom."
  • 2016: The "Blackroom" Kickstarter campaign is suspended, and the game's development is put on hold.
  • 2019: Romero releases a new set of levels for the original "Doom" called "Sigil."
  • 2021: He continues to be involved in the gaming industry, occasionally giving talks and interviews about his experiences.
